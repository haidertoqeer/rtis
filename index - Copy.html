Vaccine Preventable Diseases (Diphtheria)

Diphtheria is caused by infection with a bacterium called Corynebacteriumdiphtheriae (C. diphtheriae) or by orynebacterium ulcerans (C.ulcerans). These bacteria are usually spread in droplets of moisture coughed into the air. The bacteria then multiply in the mouth or throat of the individual who breathes them in.

Vaccine Preventable Diseases

Expanded Programme on Immunisation (EPI) aims to vaccinate children aged 0-15 months against ten Vaccine Preventable Diseases (VPDs) and pregnant women.

Polio

Polio is a highly infectious viral disease that mainly affects young children.

Diarrhoea

Rotavirus is the number one cause of diarrhea and kills around 53,300 children in Pakistan each year.

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is a lungs infection that spreads through breathing.

Pertussis

Pertussis is an infectious disease that causes violent coughing spells.

Tetanus

Tetanus is caused by bacteria found in the soil and it enters the body through open wounds.

Hepatitis-B

Hepatitis B is a contagious liver disease that results from the Hepatitis B virus.

Meningitis

Meningitis is an inflammation of the inside layers of the brain. It can be bacterial, viral or fungal.
